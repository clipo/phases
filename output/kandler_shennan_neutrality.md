# Non-equilibrium neutral-drift test (Kandler & Shennan 2013, adapted)

Basin curated set (n = 29), 10 decorated types, 6 ordinal bins along the oriented CA axis.

- Variant-survival statistic K(t): 6 of 8 types present in the first bin are still present in the last. At this coarse type resolution K(t) is near-saturated and uninformative (as expected); we test the diversity trajectory instead.

## Neutral-drift envelope for the diversity trajectory

| Ne | obs within neutral 95% envelope? | obs diversity trend (Spearman) |
|---|---|---|
| 100 | 6/6 bins | -1.00 |
| 300 | 6/6 bins | -1.00 |
| 1000 | 2/6 bins | -1.00 |

Observed per-bin diversity: 0.68, 0.67, 0.62, 0.56, 0.49, 0.37.

**Reading.** The observed diversity trajectory neither collapses (the conformist signature of diversity lost faster than drift) nor inflates beyond the neutral envelope (the anti-conformist signature of diversity maintained beyond drift); it stays within the neutral-drift envelope across the plausible range of effective size. The test cannot reject neutral transmission, concordant with the frequency increment test (seven of nine types neutral) and with the absence of a marker signature. With an ordinal axis and unknown effective size the neutral interval is wide (Kandler & Shennan 2013), so this is concordant but weak evidence; a sharper test would require the raw motif-level (variant) data rather than the ten aggregated types.
