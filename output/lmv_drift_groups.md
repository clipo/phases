# Phase-like groups under drift across the lower Mississippi Valley (n = 55)

Forward neutral drift on the river-network geography of all 55 Mainfort-PFG decorated LMV assemblages, no boundary imposed, 500 realizations. Groups are greedy-modularity communities on the Brainerd-Robinson similarity graph.

- Number of groups, contemporaneous (pure-space) sampling: mean 3.28 (range 2-5).
- Number of groups, time-transgressive sampling: mean 2.11 (range 2-3); the early-to-late repertoire turnover collapses the count toward two time-defined groups.
- Named culture-historical phases in this set: 7. Observed data cluster into 2 groups.
- Between-group cultural F_ST: observed 0.041; contemporaneous drift mean 0.024 (95% 0.012 to 0.041); time-transgressive drift mean 0.006.

Interpretation: drift structured by geography already produces a few spatially coherent, phase-like groups, fewer than the seven named phases the regional scheme draws, at about the observed level of differentiation. The named phases subdivide the valley more finely than the drift structure supports.
